Perkedel kentang (Indonesian potato fritters) is a ubiquitous everyday side dish in Indonesian, and is popular for lunch, dinner, or even as snacks! The humble perkedel is tasty and filling, and you will spot these everywhere in Indonesia, from street hawkers, tiny Mom-and-Pop family restaurants, all the way to fancy formal banquets. Interestingly, the name “perkedel” is derived from “frikandel”, a Dutch term for deep-fried minced meat patties. It becomes perkedel when we adopt it into our cuisine. Other varieties of perkedel also exist, such as perkedel tahu (tofu fritters), perkedel tempe (tempeh fritters) and perkedel jagung (corn fritters) to name a few.

What are the ingredients to prepare perkedel kentang?

  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and boiled until tender
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Boil the Potatoes:
    • Cut the peeled potatoes into chunks.
    • Boil them in salted water until they are soft and can be easily mashed with a fork, usually about 15-20 minutes.
    • Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly.
  2. Mash the Potatoes:
    • Mash the boiled potatoes while they are still warm until there are no lumps. You can use a potato masher or fork for this.
  3. Add Seasonings:
    • Add the minced garlic, chopped shallots, salt, and pepper to the mashed potatoes. Mix them well.
  4. Form Perkedel:
    • Beat the egg in a separate bowl.
    • Take a small portion of the potato mixture and shape it into a patty or ball.
    • Dip each perkedel into the beaten egg, making sure to coat it evenly.
  5. Fry Perkedel:
    • Heat cooking oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat (375°F to 400°F).
    • Carefully place the perkedel into the hot oil and fry them until they are golden brown on both sides, turning them as needed. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes per side.
  6. Drain and Serve:
    • Once the perkedel are cooked to your desired crispiness, remove them from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve:
    • Perkedel is typically served with a dipping sauce like sambal or kecap manis (sweet soy sauce). Enjoy!

Finishing

This quick method skips some traditional steps like adding scallions or leek for extra flavor or using a mortar and pestle to grind the spices, but it should give you a tasty and simplified version of perkedel. Adjust the seasonings to your taste preferences, and feel free to customize with additional ingredients if you like!

Enjoy!
